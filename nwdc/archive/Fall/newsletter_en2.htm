NKT-IKBU International Fall Festival 2010, Brazil Newsletter

The International Fall Festival 2010 is rapidly approaching. Soon we will start packing our bags and preparing for the journey to see our precious Guru, and the magnificent Kadampa World Peace Temple in Brazil. We hope that the following information will make your journey, and stay, at Kadampa Meditation Centre Brazil more comfortable. Please read carefully!

What to bring?

Don’t forget to bring your copy of Modern Buddhism!

During the Fall Festival teachings, Venerable Geshe-la will give a blessing transmission of his most recent book Modern Buddhism: The Path of Compassion and Wisdom. There will be a limited number of non-Portuguese editions for sale at the Fall Festival - so please bring a copy with you. If you do not already have a copy – please contact your local NKT Dharma Centre shop, or visit www.tharpa.com.

Tharpa Brazil will launch the Portuguese edition at the Fall Festival.

You should also bring the following sadhanas: Prayers for Meditation, Wishfulfilling Jewel, Offering to the Spiritual Guide, and The Yoga of Buddha Heruka as limited numbers of copies will be available.

Did you know that October is Springtime in Brazil (and not Fall)!

In general the weather in Sao Paulo state  in October can be fairly warm during the day (76F/24C ) and cool during the night (57F/14C on average). Spring weather is unpredictable, however, and it is possible that daytime temperatures could also be in the region of 59F/15C - and dropping lower at night.  In the course of the Festival we will probably experience a little bit of everything.  It is important therefore that you come prepared and so we recommend that in addition to bringing sun block you also bring clothes for cold, windy and wet weather (including a rain jacket).  The sun at the Temple site is strong so please bring a hat. Umbrellas can provide both protection from sun as well as rain!

The sun sets quite early – around 6.30pm – please bring a torch.

Campers - come prepared!

If the sky is clear, the temperature will drop at night – please bring a warm sleeping bag. Additional blankets will not be available. Please remember no camping stoves are allowed due to the Fire Regulations. Due to its elevated position within a valley in the Serra do Japi region, KMC Brazil can experience strong winds. Therefore it is essential that you bring all the necessary equipment for your tent. Before you come, please check that you have all pegs and guy ropes.

What not to bring?

1. Laptops
Please do not bring your laptop - you will not be able to use it on-site.

Internet provision in the local area is quite weak and KMC Brazil is regularly without internet access. Many essential Festival functions depend upon internet access but these will not be possible if the system is overloaded – please help the Festival to run smoothly by not bringing your laptop. We regret it will not possible to provide public internet access during the Festival at KMC Brazil, therefore please complete any necessary or urgent on-line work before arriving. There are limited public Internet facilities in the local town - Jacare - at a cost of approx. R$2 per hour.

2. Mobile Phones

Due to the rural location of KMC Brazil, the local mobile phone mast can only support a limited number of phones. As with internet access, mobile phone access is also essential for the smooth running of the Festival. Again, in order for there to be effective Festival communications, it is essential that this signal is not overloaded.

Please do not use your mobile phone on-site!

In addition, roaming charges in Brazil can be very high, please check with your current network provider before arriving - and ensure that you disable any ‘push’ email.

3. Any other rechargeable equipment

Electricity supply in Brazil is either 110v or 220v – standard European plugs and plug adapters will work if the appliance can accept these voltages. At Chacara dos Sonhos, only 220v is available – please check therefore that any electrical items are suitable for this.

Unfortunately we will be unable to provide public access to power points to recharge electrical equipment at the KMC site.

Health Care in Brazil

YOU MUST ARRANGE YOUR OWN HEALTHCARE INSURANCE BEFORE YOU ARRIVE

You may be aware that Brazil has a free public healthcare system. However, provision varies greatly depending on where you are residing. The medical facilities in the local town near the Temple are very limited. The only way to ensure prompt and suitable treatment should you need it, is to have private medical insurance. In many countries it is possible to purchase very cheap travel insurance which will cover for Medical Emergencies. We strongly recommend you consider this option.

If you regularly take prescription medication, please bring enough to last your whole trip. Keep some in your hand luggage in case your bags don’t make it to the airport at the same time as you do! Keep a separate written record of what you take and in what doses. Medicines have different names in different countries, it is essential that you bring a record of the generic name of your medication.
